# First Week Checklist

The first week decides whether your faction reaches its intended game. The goal is not to clear everything. The goal is to unlock growth, mines, hero levels, and movement routes without spending the army that makes week two possible.

## Day 1

- Check town build options before moving.
- Recruit useful early units, but keep enough gold for the next building.
- Scout roads, mines, loose resources, and low-risk neutral stacks.
- Fight only if the reward opens a route, resource chain, or level.

## Days 2-3

- Claim wood and ore routes unless your town has a specific rare-resource bottleneck.
- Prioritize mines over scattered piles when the mine is safe enough.
- Avoid ending turns one tile short of a known reward.
- Use the hero with the main army for fights; scouts should reveal, collect safe piles, and chain information.

## Days 4-5

- Build toward weekly creature growth.
- Decide whether your main route is a town push, mine consolidation, dwelling chain, or hero-level route.
- Visit stat and magic objects only when they do not derail the route.
- Upgrade only when the upgrade changes the next fight or protects a key stack.

## Days 6-7

- Buy before the week rolls if it affects defense or a critical fight.
- Keep enough gold to recruit important week two growth.
- Position the main hero near the next objective, not back at town unless defense or recruitment demands it.
- Review losses. If a stack disappeared, identify whether the cause was retaliation, shooter exposure, bad spell timing, or greed.

## Failure signals

- You own no new mine by day 3.
- Your main hero spends a turn moving with no reveal, collection, fight, or setup value.
- Your best stack is smaller after fighting for a minor reward.
- Your town has upgrades but lacks the dwellings or economy that create new army.
