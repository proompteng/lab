# Combat

Combat converts map decisions into permanent advantage or permanent losses. The goal is not only to win the current fight, but to preserve enough army to keep taking the next fights.

## Beginner rule

Take fights where your strongest stacks act before the enemy can punish them, and avoid fights that trade rare units for minor rewards.

## Tactical checklist

- Check initiative and who acts before your fragile stacks.
- Count retaliation before committing melee damage.
- Block shooters only when the blocking unit survives or the trade is worth it.
- Use spells before losses happen, not after the key stack is already gone.
- Protect ranged and caster stacks with bodies, terrain, or control.
- Preserve elite stacks; losing them often slows the map more than skipping a reward.

## Sieges

Sieges punish poor timing because walls, chokepoints, towers, and defender advantage can change damage math. Attack only when the reward is worth the expected losses or when delaying gives the opponent too much growth.

## Common mistake

Autopiloting neutral fights. Manual play is worth it when the reward unlocks a mine, dwelling, town timing, or hero level.
