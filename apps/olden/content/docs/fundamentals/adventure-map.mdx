# Adventure Map

The adventure map is the strategic layer: mines, towns, roads, creature dwellings, stat objects, shrines, banks, neutral armies, and objectives. Every turn asks the same question: which path creates the most future options for the least permanent army loss?

## Beginner rule

Move with a route in mind. A route should reveal information, collect resources, claim a mine, unlock a building, gain a level, or set up the next fight.

## What matters

- Roads and terrain decide how far a hero can act before the opponent.
- Mines create weekly income and are usually better than one-time piles.
- Dwellings add creature growth and change future fight math.
- Neutral stacks are not obstacles only; they are gates protecting rewards.
- Town distance matters because recruitment and defense consume movement.

## Common mistake

Clearing the nearest object just because it is near. The correct route may skip a weak reward to reach a mine, road split, or dwelling before the opponent.
